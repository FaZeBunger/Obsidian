# Freudian Theory

In **Freudian Theory** dreams have two kinds of content:
- **manifest**: The overt story line of the dream, the part the dreamer can remember and tell to others.
- **latent**: The underlying meaning or symbolism of the dream.

There are a number of problems with Freud's Theory, For one, people don't always remember their dreams, and when they do, they can't always accurately describe them. Additionally there is limited research supporting that dreams actually translate into conflict between consciousness and unconsciousness processes or unconscious wishes and desires.


# Modern Approaches

## Activation-Synthesis hypothesis

This theory suggests the experience of dreaming has no explicit or reliable meaning; rather, it is a consequence of the other processes that occur during  sleep.

## Cognitive Theory of Dreams

This theory says that dreams are essentially an outlet for your brain to review new information and freely troubleshoot issues that arose during the day, with no boundaries or consequences. 

## Evolutionary Theory of Dreams

This theory says that we dream about things directly related to survival and that these dreams can actually lead to enhanced performance when encountering real life threatening events.
