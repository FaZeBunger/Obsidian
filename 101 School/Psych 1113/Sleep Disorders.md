**Dyssomnias**: Problems with the quality of sleep.
**Parasomnias**: Disturbances that occur during sleep.

# Dyssomnias

## Insomnia

One of the most commonly diagnosed sleep disorders. Described as being unable to fall asleep and remain asleep.

**Causes**: 
- Stress
- Drinking Caffeine to late in the day
- Napping too much during waking hours
- Looking at bright screens before bed.

## Hypersomnia

In contrast with insomnia, Hypersomnia is characterized by excessive sleepiness. One of the common causes of Hypersomnia is **Sleep Apnea**.

## Sleep Apnea

Sleep Apnea is a condition where the intake of oxygen is reduced as the person sleeps, usually for only short periods of time, but on occasion for as long as a minute. In some cases breathing may become shallow, in more severe cases, the individual stops breathing entirely for short periods of time. When blood oxygen levels decrease due to lack of breathing, the brain send signals to the body to wake up and start breathing again. This cycle then repeats.

## Narcolepsy

**Narcolepsy** is a chronic sleep disorder characterized by a sudden extreme need to sleep that are uncontrollable and can last anywhere from a few seconds to a few minutes. 

Another symptom of **narcolepsy** is **cataplexy** which is the condition where the body experiences extreme muscle weakness or muscular paralysis in response to strong emotions.They can still hear, and are still conscious just cannot move. 

**Sleep Paralysis** is another symptom of **narcolepsy** which is accompanied by:
- **Hypnagogic hallucinations**: Hallucinations right before sleep.
- **Hypnopompic hallucination**: Hallucinations right before waking up.

# Parasomnias

## REM Sleep Disorder
[[Stages of Sleep#^88ea43|REM Sleep Disorder]], mentioned earlier, is one example of parasomnia

## Night Terrors

Night terrors shouldn't be confused with nightmares. Instead of having a bad dream, night terrors are a sudden fight or flight reaction that happens for no reason while you sleep, suddenly waking you up. Night terrors are common in children such as frantically screaming and waking up, etc. 

## Somnambulism 

Somnambulism more commonly known as sleep walking is another example of a parasomnia. Contrary to popular belief sleepwalkers aren't acting out their dreams, they are actually acting out routine behaviors that have been automated over time.
