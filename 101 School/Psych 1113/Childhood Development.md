## Example: Stimulus Discrimination

- **First 6 Months:** Infants can **discriminate** between **all** languages.
	- Ex. Japanese and English "R" and "L".
- **First 12 Months:** Infants can discriminate between two monkey faces an older person would say are identical.
	- Similar pattern for unfamiliar human faces.
	- Face discrimination provides basis for cross-race effect.


# Cognitive Development

- **Piaget's Theory:**
	- Children learn by exploring their environment.
- Four Stages, fixed order
	- Each stage is qualitatively different than the previous stage

- **Equilibration & Disequilibration** :
	- **Assimilation**: Applying existing schemes to new experiences.
		- **aSS** = Same Scheme
	- **Accommodation**: Change schemes if information doesn't fit.
		- **aCC** = Change Categories

- **Stage 1: Sensorimotor Stage**
	- Birth to 2 years.
	- How do babies understand the world?
	- Development of object permanence (around 7 months)
		- *More recent studies suggest as early as 3-5 months.*
