- Age 9-12 for girls; age 10-13 for boys
	- 10% of White, 25% of Black girls begin puberty *before age 8*.
	- Average age of menarche mostly stable; other indicators are not.

# The Brain

The amygdala develops before the frontal lobe. Meaning during puberty you feel the heaviness of emotions without the decision making to process all the emotions effectively.


# Emerging Adulthood

**Emerging Adulthood:** a distinct development period from 18-25, characterized by:
- Identity exploration -- trying on different roles and possibilities
- Instability -- in relationships, work, and living situations 
- Self-focus -- not selfishness, but a necessary period of figuring out who you are
- Feeling "in-between" -- not quite an adolescent, not quite an adult.

The cultural message that youth is the best time of your life is not supported by evidence.


# The Good News

- Self esteem and life satisfaction increase with age. 
- People get better at emotional regulation with age.
- Social relationships become fewer but deeper and more satisfying.
