#Psychology/Unit1 

- **Sensation vs. perception:**
	- **sensation**  - transduction of stimulus energy into neural signals;
	- **perception** - interpretation of those signals into meaningful experiences.  


- **Transduction and thresholds:** receptors convert specific energy (light, sound, chemical) into neural activity; absolute and difference thresholds govern detection and discrimination.  

- **Top-down vs. bottom-up processing:** bottom-up builds perception from sensory input; top-down uses expectations, knowledge, and context to interpret ambiguous input.  

- **Attention and selective processing:** attention allocates limited resources; automatic vs. controlled processing affects what we notice.
