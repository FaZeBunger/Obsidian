#Psychology/Unit2 

**Consciousness**: The subjective experience of your internal and external world.

**Consciousness**: is an interaction between our own subjective experiences (**awareness**) and the level of attention we are willing (or able) to dedicate to those experiences (**arousal**).

# Levels of Consciousness

- **High Level Consciousness**:  A state of purposeful. controlled processing involving high alertness and attention. 
	- Having an interesting conversation or learning a new skill.
- **Low Level Consciousness**: Processing that involves the brain on "autopilot", low alertness and attention.
	- Re-watching familiar television, daydreaming, driving the same routes over and over again. 
- **Altered States of Consciousness**: A state of "different" consciousness that is neither high nor low.
	- Use of psychoactive drugs; engaging in hypnosis or meditation.
- **Subconsciousness**: The lowest level of awareness; occurs when information is processed below the surface of consciousness. 
	- Sleeping and Dreaming; subliminal processing.
- **No Consciousness**: Complete lack of consciousness.
	- Surgical anesthesia; unconsciousness due to brain trauma; coma.
