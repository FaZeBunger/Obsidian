#Psychology/Unit2 

- **Classical conditioning**: A form of associative learning in which a previously neutral stimulus comes to elicit a response after being repeatedly paired with an unconditioned stimulus that naturally evokes that response.

- **Neutral Stimulus**: can be anything that does not have meaning or trigger a response at the beginning of the conditioning process.

- **Unconditioned** **stimulus** (**US**): A stimulus that **does** have meaning and **does** cause the response of interest. Produces an **Unconditioned Response** automatically.

- **Unconditioned** **response** (**UR**): The automatic, reflexive reaction elicited by an unconditioned stimulus.

- **Conditioned** **stimulus** (**CS**): A previously neutral stimulus that, after repeated pairing with an unconditioned stimulus, elicits a conditioned response.

- **Conditioned** **response** (**CR**): The learned response elicited by the conditioned stimulus after conditioning has occurred.
