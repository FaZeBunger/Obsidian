#Psychology/Unit2

- **Learning**: A relatively permanent change in behavior resulting from experience, not due to maturation, injury, disease, or drugs.
- **Stimulus**: Any event, object, or change in the environment that elicits a sensory or behavioral response.
- **Response**: A behavior or reaction elicited by a stimulus.
- **Consequence**: An outcome that follows a response and affects the likelihood of that response occurring again; can be neutral, reinforcing, or punishing.
- **Behaviorism**: Theoretical perspective asserting that psychology should study observable behavior and the environmental factors that influence it, rather than internal mental states.
