#Psychology/Unit1 

- **Automaticity**: many bodily and cognitive processes run without conscious control (e.g., breathing, digestion, heartbeat); this frees cognitive resources for higher-level tasks.  

- **Central vs. peripheral:** 
	- CNS = brain + spinal cord (processing, integration); 
	- PNS = nerves outside CNS (sensory input, motor output).  

- **Somatic vs. autonomic:** somatic controls voluntary muscles and sensory information; autonomic regulates involuntary functions (sympathetic = arousal/fight-or-flight; parasympathetic = rest-and-digest).  

- **Hierarchical processing:** lower-level systems handle routine/rapid tasks; higher-level systems handle complex, flexible decisions.
