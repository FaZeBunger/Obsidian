# Psychoactive Drugs
 - **Psychoactive Drugs** are substances that influence mood, thoughts, or behavior. 
 - Most work by stimulating the **dopaminergic pathways** because it triggers the release of **dopamine**. Because of this, these pathways can burn out over time. 

**Addiction**: The state of being compulsively committed to something that is psychologically or physically habit forming to such extent that stopping its use causes severe trauma.

# Dependence

Occurs when an individual requires a drug to maintain normal functioning. Should the individual refrain from taking the drug, they will experience symptoms associated with withdrawal. 

## Psycological Dependence

Occurs when the user is emotionally or mentally attached to the substance. They depend on the drug to manage emotions, interact with others, and generally feel like they are functioning on a day to day basis. 

## Physical Dependence

Occurs when the body becomes reliant on a drug; stopping its use will trigger symptoms of **withdrawal**. 

With many drugs, the withdrawal symptoms - headaches, shaking, vomiting, changes in mood, or worse - can be horribly unpleasant and can severely limit recovery from the addiction. **Drugs can be either physically addictive or only psychologically addictive, but most are both physically** _**and**_ **psychologically addictive.**


# Depressants

**Depressants** are drugs that work by slowing (or depressing) the functioning of the central nervous system. **Alcohol** is a depressant. 

Alcohol increases the effectiveness of **GABA** which is implicated with the relaxed state. It also affects your [[Parts of the Brain#^7e6ab5 |Frontal Lobe]] resulting in lowered inhibitions.

As you drink more your decision making and emotional regulation become more and more impaired. Additionally the alcohol affects your [[Parts of the Brain#^6d37a4 |Cerebellum]] resulting in lowered motor coordination.

# Stimulants

**Stimulants** are drugs that increase the activity of the nervous system. Common stimulants are drugs like caffeine, nicotine, cocaine, and amphetamines. 

Caffeine is by far the largest consumed stimulant, and while at low doses can increase energy, alertness, and creativity, at large amounts it  makes you jittery and nervous, and increases anxiety. 

Stimulants like **Adderall** are commonly prescribed for ADHD due to their ability to improve focus, concentration, and attention span. It works by increasing the availability of neurotransmitters in the brain primarily dopamine and norepinephrine.

**These drugs can have adverse effects, including increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, insomnia, anxiety, and potential for addiction or dependence with prolonged use.**

# Hallucinogens

**Hallucinogens**, also known as psychedelic drugs, influence our sensory systems and our interpretation of reality. Many people who take these report **synesthesia** which is where senses seem to blend together.
