#Psychology/Unit2 

- **Operant** **conditioning**: A type of learning in which the consequences of a behavior (reinforcement or punishment) influence the probability of that behavior’s future occurrence.
- **Reinforcement**: A consequence that increases the likelihood of the behavior it follows; can be positive (presentation of a stimulus) or negative (removal of an aversive stimulus).
- **Punishment**: A consequence that decreases the likelihood of the behavior it follows; can be positive (presentation of an aversive stimulus) or negative (removal of a desired stimulus).
- **Discriminative** **stimulus**: A stimulus that signals the availability of reinforcement or punishment contingent on a particular response, thereby influencing when the response is likely to occur.
- **Generalization**: The transfer of a learned response to stimuli that are similar to the conditioned or training stimulus.
- **Stimulus** **control**: The degree to which the presence or absence of a stimulus affects the probability of a particular response.
- **Schedules** **of** **reinforcement**: Rules that determine the timing and frequency of reinforcer delivery following a response (e.g., fixed vs. variable and ratio vs. interval distinctions).
