- The relationship between two variables is examined to determine if they are associated.

- **Correlation Coefficient** describes the correlation (+1 to -1)
	- Direction: sign of number
		- Positive (+): Varies together
		- Negative (-): Varies opposite
	- Strength: size of correlation
		- Larger relationship as coefficient moves closer to 1 in either direction.

**Correlation does not equal causation!**


APA Task Force says, "The link between violent video game exposure and aggressive behavior is one of the most studied and best established."
- Possible causes?
	- Maybe more aggressive people like playing violent video games.
	- Maybe it has to do with parenting issues.

1. Naturalistic Observation - Observing a group in their natural setting with no outside interference. 
	- We want natural behavior
	- We want the full context of the behavior
	- We can't re-create it in the lab
	- (Ex.) Kids Playing video games in an arcade. 

2. Case Study - In-depth analysis of one person or a specific group of people
	- Takes a lot of time
	- (Ex.) Deep analysis of Harris & Klebold's gaming history, journals, and behaviors.

3. Surveys 
	- (Ex.) Ask students about video game habits and aggressive thoughts/behaviors.


## Think About it!

- Can these methods: 
	- Describe?
	- Explain?
	- Predict?
	- Influence?

- Two features characterize experiments: 
	1. They have multiple groups, one of which is a control group.
		- Random assignment: all participants have an equal chance of selection for each group. 
			- Note: "quasi-experimental design" when random assignment is not possible (like gender).
	2. They manipulate some variable across the groups
		- Independent variable: the variable being manipulated
		- Dependent variable: the variable being measured

Remember: Experimental research is the only type of research that is **causal**!
