#Psychology/Unit2 

**Cues** are pieces of information that help us remember events from the past. 

**Recall Cues** are those in which you are asked to remember what you know about a given subject; they are generally weaker and more open ended. 

**Recognition cues** are those in which you are asked to indicate a correct response among several choices. The correct answer is given, you just have to identify it.

# Encoding Specificity Principle

Also known as **cue**-**dependent** **learning**. This is the principle that when you remember something you encode the surroundings of where you learned it as well. So if you study with a familiar scent then if you take a test and smell that scent you will recall the information easier on the test.

This ties into state dependent memory. Where your memory state when you learn it contributes to how well you can recall it. AKA, if you lose your keys while drunk you are more likely to remember where you placed them while drunk.
