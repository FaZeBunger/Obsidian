**Biological Rhythm**: an internal "clock" that prepares the body for daily, seasonal, and annual rhythms.
- Controls sleep and wake cycle as well as other physiological responses such as hormones, changes in blood pressure, and body temperature. 

**Diurnal**: A biological preference for being active and awake during the day. (Opposite of **Nocturnal**).

**Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN)**: The structure in the brain that sets the body's circadian clock.

Humans actually have closer to a 25 hour natural clock, but it constantly gets reset by external cues (the sun).


Two Strategies for regulating biological rhythm:
- Sunlight
- Melatonin
